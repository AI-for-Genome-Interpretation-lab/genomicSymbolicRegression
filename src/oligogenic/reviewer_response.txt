We thank the reviewer for this important observation. We agree that intra-locus epistasis, as illustrated by the GB1 fitness landscape, represents a special case that does not capture the full complexity of multi-locus interactions encountered in human genetics.

To address this limitation, we extended our analysis to three oligogenic disease datasets derived from the OLIDA database, in which causative variant combinations span distinct genes at different genomic loci. Specifically: Spinocerebellar ataxia type 17 (SCA17) involves combinations across TBP and STUB1; Familial hemophagocytic lymphohistiocytosis (FHL) involves combinations across PRF1, UNC13D, STX11, and STXBP2; and Alport syndrome involves combinations across COL4A3, COL4A4, COL4A5, and COL4A6. These genes are located on different chromosomes, representing a genuine multi-locus, genome-wide epistatic setting.

For each disease, synthetic patient cohorts were generated by implanting OLIDA-documented variant combinations into 1000 Genomes Project individuals as positive cases. A false-positive balanced noise regime was applied to ensure that no single variant is marginally discriminative, forcing models to detect co-occurrence of variants across loci.
