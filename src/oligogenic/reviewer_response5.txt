We thank the reviewer for this suggestion. We have added a runtime comparison across all benchmarked methods, reported in the Supplementary Material.

As expected, Feyn SR is slower than linear models and PLINK2, reflecting the cost of its symbolic search over the model space. However, a key observation is that Feyn's runtime does not degrade dramatically as dataset size increases: the QLattice search is bounded by the number of epochs and the pre-filtering of features rather than scaling directly with the number of samples, making it tractable for datasets of the size considered here. In contrast, methods such as exhaustive pairwise PLINK2 epistasis scanning scale quadratically with the number of variants, which becomes a bottleneck in genome-wide settings. Random Forest and gradient boosting scale more gracefully but remain black-box. Overall, Feyn occupies a practical middle ground: slower than marginal association tests, but competitive with other ML approaches and without the combinatorial blowup of exhaustive interaction search.
